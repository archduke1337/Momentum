## SOLUTIONS vs PRODUCTS MESSAGING

### Solution Pages (Hero Content)
```
PROBLEM STATEMENT
  ↓
WHY IT MATTERS (Business impact)
  ↓
OUR SOLUTION (How we solve it)
  ↓
DEPLOYMENT WORKFLOW (How we work)
  ↓
WHICH ROBOT(S) (Pixel / Orbit / Cyborg used)
  ↓
PROOF (Case studies, metrics)
  ↓
CONTACT (Talk to us)
```

### Product Pages (Secondary Content)
```
(Link from solution pages or accessed via /products)

PRODUCT NAME + TAGLINE
  ↓
USE CASES (Where it's used)
  ↓
SPECS TABLE
  ↓
ATTACHMENTS / CUSTOMIZATION
  ↓
CONTACT (For this product specifically)
```

**Key:** Product pages answer "WHAT," not "WHY"
Solution pages answer "WHY" and "HOW"

## MESSAGING TONE FOR GLOBAL AUDIENCE

**Avoid:**
- Regional colloquialisms
- India-specific references (unless highlighting manufacturing advantage)
- Startup-y language ("we're disrupting," "game-changer")
- Overly technical jargon

**Use:**
- Industry terminology (OEE, throughput, cycle time, WIP)
- Direct, professional tone
- Problem-focused language ("Here's your challenge... here's how we solve it")
- International examples/case studies

---

## CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS

1. **Problem-First Messaging** — Buyer sees their problem immediately, not your product
2. **Global Positioning** — No "Indian alternative" messaging. Be a global player.
3. **Proof of Concept** — Show deployments, even if anonymized. "Deployed in automotive plants" beats "No customers yet"
4. **Technical Credibility** — Mention ROS 2, VDA 5050, multi-sensor fusion. Appeal to engineering teams.
5. **European Partnership** — Gebroeders Doms BV is a huge credibility signal. Lead with it.
